Jewels of The Southern Sky

This image was taken from Castlepoint on the east coast of Wairarapa. Outside the small beach village it's a 'Bortle 1' area which means it's the darkest class on the Bortle scale measuring sky brightness. This is a wide field image taken using an 85mm lens (not a lot of magnification beyond the naked eye) and a star tracker to allow longer exposures. It contains many prominent features of the southern sky such as The Southern Cross (lower right), Jewel Box Cluster (just to the right of the cross), Carina Nebula (the large pink-red region to the upper left) and Coalsack Nebula (dark region centre right) as well as millions of stars of the milky way.
